Ananas Menaskai

Ananas Menaskai is a traditional South Indian dish made with pineapple, commonly prepared in the Udupi and Mangalore regions of Karnataka. It's a sweet and tangy curry with a hint of spice. Here's a simple recipe for you

Ingredients

Pineapple: 1 small (peeled, cored, and chopped)
Jaggery: 1-2 tablespoons (adjust according to sweetness preference)
Tamarind: 1 small lemon-sized ball (soaked in water)
Salt: to taste
Turmeric powder: 1/4 teaspoon

For Masala (Spice Paste)
Dry red chilies: 4-5 (adjust according to spice preference)
Coriander seeds: 1 tablespoon
Fenugreek seeds: 1/4 teaspoon
Mustard seeds: 1/2 teaspoon
Urad dal (black gram): 1 tablespoon
Sesame seeds: 1 tablespoon
Grated coconut: 1/2 cup (fresh or desiccated)
Oil: 1 teaspoon (for roasting)

For Tempering
Oil: 1 tablespoon
Mustard seeds: 1/2 teaspoon
Curry leaves: 1 sprig
Asafoetida (hing): a pinch

Process

1.Prepare the Masala Paste:
Heat 1 teaspoon of oil in a pan and roast the dry red chilies, coriander seeds, fenugreek seeds, mustard seeds, and urad dal until they turn golden brown and aromatic.
Add the sesame seeds and grated coconut, and roast for another minute. Allow the mixture to cool.
Grind the roasted ingredients into a smooth paste with a little water. Set aside.

2.Cook the Pineapple:
In a deep pan, add the chopped pineapple, jaggery, tamarind water, salt, and turmeric powder.
Mix well.
Cook on medium heat until the pineapple becomes tender and the raw smell of tamarind disappears.

3.Combine the Masala Paste and Pineapple:
Add the ground masala paste to the cooked pineapple. Mix well and cook for another 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally. Adjust the consistency with water if needed.

4.Tempering:
Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a small pan. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter. Add curry leaves and a pinch of asafoetida. Pour this tempering over the pineapple curry.

5.Serve:
Mix well and serve Ananas Menaskai hot with rice, chapati, or dosa.

Avial

Avial is a popular dish from South India, known for its combination of vegetables and coconut. It's a simple, nutritious, and flavorful dish often served with rice. Here's a basic recipe

Ingredients

Mixed Vegetables (about 3 cups, cut into 2-inch pieces):
Common vegetables used include:
Carrot
Potato
Green beans
Drumstick
Raw banana
Yam
Eggplant
Grated Coconut: 1 cup
Green Chilies: 2-3, chopped
Cumin Seeds: 1 tsp
Curd (Yogurt): 1/2 cup (optional)
Coconut Oil: 2 tbsp
Curry Leaves: A few Turmeric Powder: 1/2 tsp
Salt: To taste
Water: As needed

Process

1.Prepare the Vegetables:
Peel and chop the vegetables into 2-inch pieces. You can use any combination of the vegetables listed or any other of your choice.

2.Cook the Vegetables:
In a large pot, add the vegetables, turmeric powder, salt, and enough water to cover them. Cook until the vegetables are tender but not mushy.

3.Make the Coconut Paste:
In a blender, grind the grated coconut, green chilies, and cumin seeds into a smooth paste using a little water.

4.Combine:
Once the vegetables are cooked, add the coconut paste to the pot and mix well. Cook for a few more minutes on low heat.

5.Add Curd (optional):
If using, whisk the curd and add it to the mixture. Stir gently and cook for another minute. Do not boil after adding curd.

Season with Coconut Oil:
Turn off the heat and drizzle the coconut oil over the avial. Add the curry leaves and mix gently.

Serve:
Avial is best served with steamed rice or as a side dish with other South Indian meals.

Aval Kesari

Aval Kesari is a delicious and easy-to-make Indian sweet dish made with beaten rice (also known as aval or poha), sugar, ghee, and flavored with saffron and cardamom. Here's a simple recipe

Ingredients

Aval (Beaten Rice/Poha): 1 cup
Sugar: 1 cup
Water: 1 1/2 cups
Ghee (Clarified Butter): 2-3 tbsp
Cashews: 8-10, broken
Raisins: 8-10
Cardamom Powder: 1/4 tsp
Saffron Strands: A pinch (optional)
Food Color: A pinch (optional, usually orange or yellow)


Process


1.Roast Aval:
In a pan, dry roast the aval on medium heat until it becomes crisp. This should take about 5-7 minutes. Set aside to cool and then grind it into a coarse powder. You can keep it slightly coarse or fine as per your preference.

2.Fry Cashews and Raisins:
In the same pan, heat 1 tbsp of ghee. Fry the cashews until they turn golden brown, then add the raisins and fry until they puff up. Remove and set aside.

3.Boil Water and Add Sugar:
In the pan, bring 1 1/2 cups of water to a boil. Add the sugar and stir until it dissolves completely. If you're using saffron, soak the strands in a little warm water and add them to the sugar syrup. You can also add a pinch of food color at this stage if using.

4.Add Roasted Aval:
Once the sugar has dissolved and the water is boiling, add the powdered aval gradually while stirring continuously to avoid lumps.

5.Cook:
Reduce the heat and cook the mixture until it thickens and the aval is cooked well. This should take about 5-7 minutes. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.

6.Add Ghee and Cardamom:
Once the mixture thickens, add the remaining ghee and cardamom powder. Mix well.

7.Garnish:
Add the fried cashews and raisins. Mix well and remove from heat.

8.Serve:
Aval Kesari can be served warm or at room temperature. It's a perfect sweet treat for any occasion.

Enjoy your Aval Kesari! It's a delightful, easy-to-make sweet dish that's perfect for festivals and celebrations.

Baida Roti

Baida Roti is a popular street food in India, especially in Mumbai. It's a delicious stuffed flatbread filled with a mixture of minced meat (chicken, mutton, or even keema), eggs, and spices. Here's a simple recipe to make Baida Roti at home

Ingredients

For the Dough

All-Purpose Flour (Maida): 1 cup
Water: As needed
Salt: A pinch
Oil: 1 tbsp

For the Filling

Minced Meat (Chicken, Mutton, or Keema): 200g
Onion: 1 small, finely chopped
Green Chilies: 1-2, finely chopped
Ginger-Garlic Paste: 1 tsp
Coriander Powder: 1 tsp
Cumin Powder: 1/2 tsp
Garam Masala: 1/2 tsp
Red Chili Powder: 1/2 tsp (optional)
Salt: To taste
Eggs: 2, beaten
Coriander Leaves: A handful, finely chopped
Oil: For cooking

Process

Prepare the Dough

1.Mix Flour and Salt:
In a bowl, mix the flour, salt, and oil. Gradually add water and knead into a soft, smooth dough. Cover and let it rest for 15-20 minutes.

Prepare the Filling

2.Cook the Minced Meat:
Heat a little oil in a pan. Add the chopped onions and sauté until golden brown. Add the ginger-garlic paste and cook for another minute.

3.Add Spices:
Add the minced meat, green chilies, coriander powder, cumin powder, garam masala, red chili powder (if using), and salt. Cook until the meat is cooked through and any excess water has evaporated. Stir in the chopped coriander leaves and set aside to cool.

Assemble the Baida Roti

4.Roll Out the Dough:
Divide the dough into small balls. Roll each ball into a thin circle, about 6 inches in diameter.

5.Add the Filling:
Place a portion of the cooked meat mixture in the center of the rolled dough. Pour a tablespoon of the beaten egg over the filling.

6.Fold the Roti:
Fold the edges of the dough over the filling to make a square or rectangular parcel. Ensure the filling is sealed well inside.

7.Cook the Baida Roti:
Heat a little oil in a pan over medium heat. Place the folded roti on the pan and cook until golden brown and crispy on both sides. You can add a little more oil if needed.

Serve

8.Serve Hot:
Baida Roti is best enjoyed hot, with a side of chutney, ketchup, or any dip of your choice.

Enjoy your Baida Roti! It's a flavorful and satisfying dish, perfect as a snack or a meal.

Bisibele Bath

Bisibele Bath is a traditional South Indian dish from Karnataka, known for its comforting blend of rice, lentils, vegetables, and spices. It's a wholesome, flavorful, and slightly tangy dish often enjoyed with a side of yogurt or raita. Here's a simple recipe/p>

Ingredients

For the Rice and Lentil Base

Rice: 1 cup
Toor Dal (Split Pigeon Peas): 1/2 cup
Turmeric Powder: 1/4 tsp
Water: As needed

For the Vegetables

Mixed Vegetables: 2 cups (carrot, potato, peas, beans, bell pepper, etc.)
Tomato: 1, chopped
Tamarind Paste: 2 tbsp
Salt: To taste
Water: As needed

For the Bisibele Bath Powder

Dry Red Chilies: 4-5
Coriander Seeds: 1 tbsp
Chana Dal (Bengal Gram): 1 tsp
Urad Dal (Split Black Gram): 1 tsp
Cinnamon Stick: 1 small piece
Cloves: 2-3
Fenugreek Seeds: 1/4 tsp
Cumin Seeds: 1/2 tsp
Poppy Seeds: 1/2 tsp (optional)
Desiccated Coconut: 1 tbsp (optional)

For Tempering

Ghee or Oil: 2 tbsp
Mustard Seeds: 1 tsp
Curry Leaves: A few
Asafoetida (Hing): A pinch
Cashews: A few (optional)

Process


Prepare the Rice and Lentils

1.Cook the Rice and Dal:
Rinse the rice and toor dal. Cook them together with turmeric powder and enough water in a pressure cooker or pot until soft. Set aside.

Prepare the Vegetables

2.Cook the Vegetables:
In another pot, boil the mixed vegetables with water until tender. Add the chopped tomato and cook until soft. Add tamarind paste, salt, and a little water. Let it simmer.

Make the Bisibele Bath Powder

3.Roast the Spices:
In a small pan, dry roast the dry red chilies, coriander seeds, chana dal, urad dal, cinnamon, cloves, fenugreek seeds, cumin seeds, and poppy seeds (if using) until golden and fragrant.
4.Grind the Spices:
Once cooled, grind the roasted spices along with desiccated coconut (if using) into a fine powder. This is your Bisibele Bath powder.

Combine and Cook

5. Mix Everything:
Add the cooked rice and dal mixture to the pot with the vegetables. Add the prepared Bisibele Bath powder and mix well. Add water if needed to reach your desired consistency. Let it simmer for a few minutes until everything is well combined and hot.

Tempering

6. Prepare the Tempering:
In a small pan, heat ghee or oil. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter. Add curry leaves, asafoetida, and cashews (if using). Fry until the cashews are golden.
7.Add the Tempering:
Pour the tempering over the Bisibele Bath and mix well.

Serve

8.Serve Hot: Bisibele Bath is best served hot, garnished with fresh coriander leaves. It's often enjoyed with a side of yogurt, raita, or potato chips.

Enjoy your Bisibele Bath! It's a nutritious and flavorful dish perfect for lunch or dinner.

Bonda

Bonda is a popular South Indian snack, typically made from spiced mashed potatoes that are coated in a gram flour batter and deep-fried. There are variations like Aloo Bonda (potato filling), Mysore Bonda (made with urad dal), and more. Here's a simple recipe for the classic Aloo Bonda

Ingredients

For the Filling

Potatoes: 3-4 medium-sized, boiled and mashed
Onion: 1 small, finely chopped
Green Chilies: 1-2, finely chopped
Ginger: 1 tsp, finely chopped
Curry Leaves: A few, chopped
Mustard Seeds: 1 tsp
Cumin Seeds: 1/2 tsp
Turmeric Powder: 1/4 tsp
Red Chili Powder: 1/2 tsp (optional)
Salt: To taste
Coriander Leaves: A handful, finely chopped
Oil: 1-2 tsp (for tempering)

For the Batter

Besan (Gram Flour): 1 cup
Rice Flour: 2 tbsp (optional, for extra crispiness)
Red Chili Powder: 1/2 tsp
Turmeric Powder: A pinch
Baking Soda: A pinch (optional)
Salt: To taste
Water: As needed
Oil: For deep frying

Process


Prepare the Filling

1.Temper the Spices:
Heat 1-2 tsp of oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter. Add cumin seeds, chopped ginger, green chilies, curry leaves, and chopped onions. Sauté until the onions are soft and translucent.

2.Add Spices and Potatoes:
Add turmeric powder, red chili powder (if using), and salt. Mix well. Add the mashed potatoes and mix thoroughly. Cook for a couple of minutes until everything is well combined. Stir in the chopped coriander leaves.

3.Shape the Filling:
Once the filling mixture cools down, shape it into small balls, about the size of a golf ball. Set aside.

Prepare the Batter

4.Mix the Batter:
In a mixing bowl, combine besan, rice flour (if using), red chili powder, turmeric powder, baking soda (if using), and salt. Gradually add water to form a smooth, thick batter. The batter should be thick enough to coat the potato balls without dripping too much. Fry the Bondas

5.Heat Oil:
Heat oil in a deep frying pan or kadai over medium heat.

6.Coat and Fry:
Dip each potato ball into the batter, ensuring it is well coated. Carefully drop it into the hot oil. Fry in batches, turning occasionally, until the bondas are golden brown and crispy.

7.Drain:
Remove the fried bondas with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.

Serve

8.Serve Hot:
Bondas are best served hot with chutney, ketchup, or any dip of your choice.

Enjoy your delicious, crispy Bonda! It's a perfect snack for tea time or as an appetizer for any meal.

Briyani

Biryani is a flavorful and aromatic rice dish that's a favorite in many parts of India and beyond. It's a complete meal made with basmati rice, meat (chicken, mutton, or seafood), or vegetables, along with a rich blend of spices. Here's a basic recipe for Chicken Biryani, which you can adapt for other proteins or make vegetarian.

Ingredients

For the Rice

Basmati Rice: 2 cups
Water: 8 cups
Bay Leaf: 1
Cloves: 3-4
Green Cardamoms: 2-3
Cinnamon Stick: 1 small piece
Salt: 1 tsp

For the Chicken Marinade

Chicken: 500g, cut into pieces
Yogurt: 1/2 cup
Ginger-Garlic Paste: 1 tbsp
Red Chili Powder: 1 tsp
Turmeric Powder: 1/2 tsp
Garam Masala: 1 tsp
Salt: To taste
Lemon Juice: 1 tbsp

For the Biryani Masala

Onions: 2 large, thinly sliced
Tomatoes: 2, chopped
Green Chilies: 2, slit
Ginger-Garlic Paste: 1 tbsp
Biryani Masala Powder: 2 tbsp (or a mix of garam masala and ground spices)
Fresh Coriander Leaves: A handful, chopped
Fresh Mint Leaves: A handful, chopped
Saffron: A pinch (soaked in 2 tbsp warm milk)
Ghee or Oil: 3-4 tbsp
Salt: To taste

For Garnish

Fried Onions: A handful
Fresh Coriander Leaves: Chopped
Fresh Mint Leaves: Chopped

Process


Prepare the Rice

1.Cook the Rice:
Wash and soak the basmati rice for 30 minutes. In a large pot, bring water to a boil. Add bay leaf, cloves, cardamoms, cinnamon, and salt. Add the rice and cook until it's 70-80% done (the rice should still have a bite to it). Drain and set aside.

Marinate the Chicken

2.Marinate:
In a bowl, mix chicken with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, salt, and lemon juice. Marinate for at least 30 minutes or up to 4 hours in the refrigerator.

Prepare the Biryani Masala

3.Fry Onions:
In a large pan, heat ghee or oil. Add the thinly sliced onions and fry until golden brown. Remove half of the fried onions and set aside for garnish.

4.Cook the Masala:
In the same pan with the remaining onions, add green chilies and ginger-garlic paste. Sauté until the raw smell goes away. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften and the oil separates.

5.Add Spices and Chicken:
Add the biryani masala powder (or your spice mix), fresh coriander, and mint leaves. Mix well. Add the marinated chicken and cook until the chicken is 70-80% done. The chicken should still be juicy and not fully cooked, as it will finish cooking with the rice.

Layer the Biryani

6.Layer the Biryani:
In a large, heavy-bottomed pot, layer half of the cooked rice. Add the chicken and masala mixture on top. Layer the remaining rice over the chicken. Drizzle the saffron milk on top. Garnish with fried onions, chopped coriander, and mint leaves.

7.Dum Cooking:
Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid. You can seal the edges with dough to ensure the steam doesn't escape. Cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes (this method is called "dum" cooking) until the flavors meld together and the rice is fully cooked. Alternatively, you can place the pot in a preheated oven at 180°C (350°F) for 25-30 minutes.

Serve

8.Fluff and Serve:
Gently fluff the rice with a fork, mixing the layers. Serve hot with raita, salad, or any side dish of your choice.

Enjoy your delicious Chicken Biryani! It's a dish full of rich flavors and aromas, perfect for special occasions or a comforting meal at home.

Halwa

Halwa is a popular Indian dessert known for its rich, sweet, and comforting flavor. There are various types of halwa, such as sooji (semolina) halwa, gajar (carrot) halwa, and besan (gram flour) halwa. Here's a simple recipe for Sooji Halwa (Semolina Halwa), which is one of the most common and easy-to-make halwas.

Ingredients

Sooji (Semolina/Rava): 1 cup
Ghee (Clarified Butter): 1/2 cup
Sugar: 1 cup
Water: 2 1/2 cups
Milk: 1/2 cup (optional, for richer taste)
Cardamom Powder: 1/2 tsp
Cashews: 8-10
Raisins: 8-10
Saffron Strands: A pinch (optional)
Almonds/Pistachios: A few, chopped (optional, for garnish)

Process


Prepare the Syrup

1.Boil Water and Sugar:
In a pot, combine water, milk (if using), and sugar. Bring it to a boil until the sugar dissolves completely. Add saffron strands (if using) and set aside.

Roast the Semolina

2.Roast Sooji:
In a separate pan, heat ghee on medium heat. Add the sooji and roast it, stirring continuously until it turns golden brown and aromatic. This may take 7-10 minutes. Be careful not to burn it.

Add the Syrup

3.Add the Syrup:
Once the sooji is well-roasted, carefully pour the hot sugar syrup into the pan, stirring continuously to avoid lumps. The mixture will bubble up, so add the syrup gradually and stir carefully.

Cook the Halwa

4.Cook:
Continue to cook the mixture on medium heat, stirring continuously until the sooji absorbs all the liquid and reaches a thick, pudding-like consistency. Add cardamom powder and mix well.

Fry the Nuts

5.Fry Cashews and Raisins:
In a small pan, heat a little ghee and fry the cashews until golden brown. Add the raisins and fry until they puff up. Add these to the halwa and mix well.

Serve

6.Serve Hot:
Garnish with chopped almonds or pistachios if desired. Serve the halwa hot or warm.

Enjoy your Sooji Halwa! It's a delicious and comforting dessert that's perfect for any occasion, from festive celebrations to a simple treat at home.

Chettinad Chicken

Chettinad Chicken is a flavorful and spicy South Indian chicken curry from the Chettinad region of Tamil Nadu. It's known for its aromatic spices and rich, intense flavors. Here's a simple recipe for making Chettinad Chicken at home.

Ingredients

For the Chicken Marinade :

Chicken: 500g, cut into pieces
Turmeric Powder: 1/2 tsp
Red Chili Powder: 1 tsp
Salt: To taste
Lemon Juice: 1 tbsp

For the Chettinad Masala :

Coriander Seeds: 2 tbsp

Cumin Seeds: 1 tsp
Fennel Seeds: 1 tsp
Black Peppercorns: 1 tsp
Dry Red Chilies: 4-5
Cinnamon Stick: 1 small piece
Cloves: 3-4
Cardamom Pods: 2-3
Star Anise: 1
Poppy Seeds: 1 tsp (optional)
Grated Coconut: 1/4 cup
Oil: 1-2 tsp for roasting

For the Curry :

Onions: 2 large, finely chopped

Tomatoes: 2, chopped
Ginger-Garlic Paste: 1 tbsp
Curry Leaves: A few
Turmeric Powder: 1/2 tsp
Salt: To taste
Oil: 3-4 tbsp
Fresh Coriander Leaves: A handful, chopped (for garnish)

Process


Marinate the Chicken

1. Marinate the Chicken:
In a bowl, mix the chicken pieces with turmeric powder, red chili powder, salt, and lemon juice. Marinate for at least 30 minutes, or up to 2 hours in the refrigerator.

Prepare the Chettinad Masala

2. Roast the Spices:
In a pan, heat 1-2 tsp of oil. Add coriander seeds, cumin seeds, fennel seeds, black peppercorns, dry red chilies, cinnamon stick, cloves, cardamom pods, star anise, and poppy seeds (if using). Roast until fragrant and golden.

3. Add Coconut:
Add the grated coconut to the pan and roast until it turns golden brown. Stir continuously to prevent burning.

4. Grind the Masala:
Let the roasted spices and coconut cool, then grind them into a fine powder or paste using a little water. This is your Chettinad masala.

Cook the Curry

5. Sauté Onions and Spices:
In a large pan or kadai, heat 3-4 tbsp of oil. Add the finely chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.

6. Add Ginger-Garlic Paste:
Add the ginger-garlic paste and curry leaves. Sauté until the raw smell disappears.

7. Add Tomatoes:
Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft and the oil starts to separate from the masala.

8. Add Masala:
Add the prepared Chettinad masala and turmeric powder. Mix well and cook for a few minutes until the masala is well combined and aromatic.

Cook the Chicken

9. Add Chicken:
Add the marinated chicken pieces to the pan. Stir to coat the chicken with the masala. Cook on medium heat until the chicken is well browned.
10. Simmer:
Add a little water to create a thick gravy. Cover the pan and let the chicken simmer on low heat until it's fully cooked and tender. Stir occasionally and add more water if needed to reach your desired consistency. 11. Season: Adjust salt to taste.
Serve

12. Garnish and Serve:
Garnish the Chettinad Chicken with freshly chopped coriander leaves. Serve hot with steamed rice, biryani, roti, or naan.


Enjoy your Chettinad Chicken! It's a spicy, aromatic dish that's full of bold flavors, perfect for those who love a bit of heat in their food.

Golibajje

Golibajje, also known as Mangalore Bajji or Mysore Bonda, is a popular South Indian snack, especially in Karnataka. These are fluffy, deep-fried fritters made with a spiced batter of all-purpose flour and yogurt. They're crispy on the outside and soft inside, making them a perfect tea-time snack.

Ingredients

All-Purpose Flour (Maida): 1 cup
Rice Flour: 1-2 tbsp (for extra crispiness)
Yogurt (Curd): 1/2 cup
Green Chilies: 2, finely chopped
Ginger: 1-inch piece, finely chopped
Curry Leaves: A few, finely chopped
Cumin Seeds: 1/2 tsp
Baking Soda: 1/4 tsp
Asafoetida (Hing): A pinch
Salt: To taste
Sugar: 1/2 tsp (optional, for a hint of sweetness)
Water: As needed
Oil: For deep frying

Process


Prepare the Batter

1. Mix Dry Ingredients:
In a mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour, rice flour, salt, sugar (if using), cumin seeds, baking soda, and asafoetida.

2. Add Wet Ingredients:
Add yogurt, finely chopped green chilies, ginger, and curry leaves to the dry ingredients.

3. Make the Batter:
Mix well and gradually add water as needed to make a thick, sticky batter. The consistency should be thick enough to hold its shape when dropped into oil but not too stiff.

4. Rest the Batter:
Let the batter rest for about 15-20 minutes. This helps in making the bajjis soft and fluffy.

Fry the Golibajje

5. Heat Oil:
In a deep frying pan or kadai, heat oil on medium heat. The oil should be hot but not smoking.<

6. Drop Batter:
Once the oil is hot, take small portions of the batter (you can use your fingers or a spoon) and drop them carefully into the hot oil. Fry in batches, ensuring not to overcrowd the pan.

7. Fry Until Golden:
Fry the golibajje until they turn golden brown and crispy on all sides. Make sure the oil is at a consistent medium heat so that the inside cooks well without burning the outside.

8. Drain:
Remove the fried golibajje with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.

Serve

9. Serve Hot:
Serve golibajje hot with coconut chutney, tomato ketchup, or any dip of your choice.


Enjoy your Golibajje! These fluffy, flavorful fritters are perfect for enjoying with a cup of hot tea or coffee.

Ennai kathirikkai (or) Oil Brinjal

Ennai Kathirikkai or Oil Brinjal is a delicious South Indian dish, especially popular in Tamil Nadu. It features tender baby brinjals (eggplants) cooked in a rich, spicy, and tangy tamarind gravy, with a generous amount of oil, which gives the dish its name. It’s often served with rice or biryani.

Ingredients

For the Spice Paste

Coriander Seeds: 1 tbsp
Cumin Seeds: 1 tsp
Fennel Seeds: 1 tsp
Dry Red Chilies: 4-5
Black Peppercorns: 1/2 tsp
Chana Dal (Bengal Gram): 1 tbsp
Urad Dal (Black Gram): 1 tbsp
Grated Coconut: 2 tbsp
Sesame Seeds: 1 tsp (optional)
Oil: 1 tsp for roasting

For the Gravy

Baby Brinjals (Eggplants): 8-10, slit crosswise, keeping the stems intact
Tamarind Pulp: 1/2 cup (soak a lemon-sized ball of tamarind in warm water and extract the juice)
Turmeric Powder: 1/2 tsp
Salt: To taste
Jaggery: 1 tsp (optional, for a slight sweetness)
Curry Leaves: A few
Mustard Seeds: 1/2 tsp
Asafoetida (Hing): A pinch
Oil: 4-5 tbsp (traditionally sesame oil is used)

Process


Prepare the Spice Paste

1. Roast the Spices:
Heat 1 tsp of oil in a pan. Add coriander seeds, cumin seeds, fennel seeds, dry red chilies, black peppercorns, chana dal, and urad dal. Roast until they turn golden brown and fragrant.

2. Add Coconut:
Add the grated coconut and sesame seeds (if using) to the pan and roast for another minute until the coconut turns golden.

3. Grind to a Paste:
Allow the roasted mixture to cool, then grind it into a fine paste using a little water. Set aside.

Cook the Brinjals

4. Fry the Brinjals:
In a heavy-bottomed pan, heat 3-4 tbsp of oil. Add the slit brinjals and fry them on medium heat until they are partially cooked and slightly browned on the outside. Remove and set aside.

Prepare the Gravy

5. Temper the Spices:
In the same pan, add a little more oil if needed. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter. Add asafoetida and curry leaves, and sauté for a few seconds.

6. Cook the Gravy:
Add the ground spice paste to the pan and sauté it in the oil until the raw smell goes away and the oil begins to separate. This may take a few minutes.

7. Add Tamarind and Spices:
Pour in the tamarind pulp, turmeric powder, salt, and jaggery (if using). Mix well and bring it to a boil.

8. Simmer with Brinjals:
Add the fried brinjals to the tamarind gravy. Cover and simmer on low heat until the brinjals are fully cooked and tender, and the oil starts to float on top. Adjust the consistency by adding a little water if needed.

Serve

9. Serve Hot:
Ennai Kathirikkai is ready to serve. Garnish with fresh curry leaves and serve hot with steamed rice, biryani, or chapati.


Enjoy your Ennai Kathirikkai! This dish is a perfect blend of tangy, spicy, and rich flavors that pair beautifully with a variety of South Indian meals.

Hyderabad Briyani

Hyderabadi Biryani is a world-famous dish from Hyderabad, India, known for its rich aroma, exquisite taste, and the unique method of cooking called "Dum" cooking. It’s made with fragrant basmati rice, marinated meat (usually chicken or mutton), and a blend of aromatic spices. There are two main types of Hyderabadi Biryani: Kacchi (raw) Biryani and Pakki (cooked) Biryani. Here’s a simple recipe for Hyderabadi Chicken Biryani (Kacchi Style), where raw marinated chicken is layered with partially cooked rice and cooked together.

Ingredients

For the Chicken Marinade

Chicken: 500g, cut into pieces
Yogurt: 1/2 cup
Ginger-Garlic Paste: 2 tbsp
Red Chili Powder: 1-2 tsp
Turmeric Powder: 1/2 tsp
Coriander Powder: 1 tsp
Garam Masala Powder: 1 tsp
Green Chilies: 3-4, slit
Mint Leaves: 1/2 cup, chopped
Coriander Leaves: 1/2 cup, chopped
Lemon Juice: 2 tbsp
Fried Onions: 1/2 cup (golden brown, crispy)
Salt: To taste
Oil: 2 tbsp
Saffron: A few strands soaked in 2 tbsp warm milk (for color)

For the Rice

Basmati Rice: 2 cups (long-grain)
Water: 6-8 cups (for boiling rice)
Whole Spices: 1 bay leaf, 4 cloves, 4 green cardamoms, 1-inch cinnamon stick
Salt: To taste
Oil/Ghee: 1 tbsp

For Layering and Cooking

Fried Onions: 1/2 cup (additional for layering)
Ghee: 2 tbsp
Milk: 1/4 cup (optional, to drizzle)
Mint and Coriander Leaves: A handful, chopped (for garnish)
Saffron Milk: Prepared earlier
Oil/Ghee: 1-2 tbsp (to drizzle on top)

Process


Marinate the Chicken

1. Marinate the Chicken:
In a large bowl, mix the chicken pieces with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, coriander powder, garam masala powder, slit green chilies, chopped mint and coriander leaves, lemon juice, fried onions, salt, and oil.

2. Rest:
Marinate for at least 2 hours, or overnight in the refrigerator for better flavor.

Prepare the Rice

3. Soak the Rice:
Wash and soak the basmati rice in water for 30 minutes, then drain.

4. Cook the Rice:
In a large pot, bring 6-8 cups of water to a boil. Add whole spices (bay leaf, cloves, cardamoms, cinnamon stick), salt, and 1 tbsp oil/ghee.

5. Parboil the Rice:
Add the soaked rice to the boiling water and cook until the rice is 70% cooked (grains should still be slightly firm). Drain the water and set the rice aside.

Layer the Biryani

6. Layering:
In a heavy-bottomed pot or handi, add a layer of marinated chicken at the bottom.

7. Top with Rice:
Spread the parboiled rice evenly over the chicken.

8. Garnish and Drizzle:
Sprinkle fried onions, chopped mint, and coriander leaves on top. Drizzle saffron milk, ghee, and a little milk if using. You can also drizzle some oil or ghee over the top for extra flavor.

Dum Cooking (Steaming)

9. Seal the Pot:
Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid. If the lid isn’t tight enough, you can seal the edges with dough to trap the steam inside.

Cook on Dum

10. High Heat:
Place the pot on high heat for 5 minutes to generate steam. 11. Low Heat: Reduce the heat to low and cook for 25-30 minutes. Alternatively, place the pot on a heated tawa (griddle) to avoid direct heat and prevent burning.

12. Check for Doneness:
After 30 minutes, turn off the heat and let it rest for 10 minutes. Carefully open the lid and check if the chicken is cooked and the rice is tender.

Serve

13. Serve Hot:
Gently fluff the rice with a fork, being careful not to break the grains. Serve the biryani hot with raita, salan (gravy), or a boiled egg on the side.

Enjoy your Hyderabadi Chicken Biryani! This dish is a perfect combination of aromatic spices, tender chicken, and fluffy rice, making it a must-try for any biryani lover.

Idiyappam

Idiyappam, also known as String Hoppers, is a traditional South Indian and Sri Lankan dish made from rice flour. It is light, fluffy, and often served with coconut-based curries, stew, or sweetened coconut milk. It's a simple dish, but the texture and delicate strands make it unique and delicious.

Ingredients

Rice Flour: 1 cup (preferably roasted)
Water: 1 to 1.5 cups (boiling hot)
Salt: To taste
Oil: 1 tsp

Process


Prepare the Dough

1. Boil Water:
In a pan, bring water to a rolling boil. Add salt and 1 tsp of oil to the boiling water.

2. Mix Dough:
In a mixing bowl, place the rice flour. Gradually add the boiling water to the rice flour while stirring continuously with a wooden spoon or spatula. Mix until it forms a smooth, soft dough. The dough should not be too stiff or too soft.

3. Knead:
Once the dough is cool enough to handle, knead it briefly to ensure it’s smooth and lump-free.

Make the Idiyappam

4. Prepare the Idiyappam Press:
Grease the idiyappam press (a device similar to a noodle or pasta maker, with a fine holed disc) with a little oil. Fill it with the dough.

5. Press the Dough:
Press the dough through the idiyappam press directly onto greased idli plates, banana leaves, or any flat steamer plate in small circular motions to create delicate, thin strands of dough.

6.Steam the Idiyappam:
Place the plates in a steamer or idli cooker and steam for about 10-12 minutes or until the idiyappam is cooked. They should become slightly translucent when done.

Serve

7. Serve Hot:
Serve the idiyappam hot with coconut milk sweetened with sugar or jaggery, coconut chutney, vegetable stew, or any curry of your choice.


Enjoy your Idiyappam! This light and versatile dish can be enjoyed with both sweet and savory accompaniments, making it perfect for any meal.

Double Ka Meetha

Double Ka Meetha is a traditional Hyderabadi dessert made from bread, milk, sugar, and ghee, flavored with cardamom and garnished with nuts. The name "Double Ka Meetha" refers to the bread used in the recipe, which is often called "double roti" in Hyderabad, meaning bread that has doubled in size.

Ingredients

Bread Slices: 6-8 (white bread or milk bread)
Milk: 2 cups
Sugar: 1 cup (adjust to taste)
Ghee: 4-5 tbsp (for frying)
Cardamom Powder: 1/2 tsp
Saffron: A few strands (soaked in 2 tbsp warm milk)
Nuts: 2 tbsp (almonds, cashews, and pistachios, chopped)
Raisins: 1 tbsp (optional)
Water: 1/2 cup (for sugar syrup)
Rose Water or Kewra Water: 1 tsp (optional, for flavor)

Process


Prepare the Bread

1. Toast the Bread:
Cut the bread slices into halves or quarters, removing the crust if preferred. Heat 2-3 tbsp of ghee in a frying pan, and fry the bread pieces until they are golden brown and crispy on both sides. Set them aside on a paper towel to drain excess ghee.

Toast the Bread:>

2. Prepare Sugar Syrup:
In a saucepan, combine sugar and water. Bring to a boil, stirring until the sugar dissolves completely. Add cardamom powder and rose water or kewra water (if using) for added flavor. Once the syrup is slightly thickened, remove from heat and set aside.

Cook the Milk

3. Boil the Milk:
In another pan, bring the milk to a boil. Lower the heat and simmer for a few minutes, stirring occasionally until the milk reduces slightly and thickens. Add the saffron-soaked milk to the mixture and stir well.

Assemble the Double Ka Meetha

4. Layer the Bread:
Arrange the fried bread pieces in a shallow dish or pan.

5. Add the Syrup and Milk:>
Pour the warm sugar syrup evenly over the bread slices, ensuring they soak up the syrup. Then, pour the thickened milk over the bread.

6. Garnish:
Sprinkle the chopped nuts and raisins (if using) over the top.

Cook and Serve

7. Simmer or Bake:
Cover the dish and simmer on low heat for 10-15 minutes until the bread absorbs the milk and flavors. Alternatively, you can bake it in a preheated oven at 180°C (350°F) for about 20 minutes until the top is slightly golden and bubbly.

8. Serve Warm:
Double Ka Meetha is best served warm, either as it is or with a scoop of ice cream on the side for an extra indulgence.


Enjoy your Double Ka Meetha! This rich, flavorful dessert is a perfect treat for special occasions or whenever you crave something sweet and satisfying.

Dibba Rotti

Dibba Rotti is a traditional Andhra dish, also known as Minapa Rotti. It’s a thick, crispy pancake made from urad dal (black gram) and rice rava (coarse rice flour). This dish is often enjoyed as a breakfast item or a snack and pairs well with chutneys or pickle.

Ingredients

Urad Dal (Black Gram): 1 cup
Rice Rava (Coarse Rice Flour): 2 cups
Cumin Seeds: 1 tsp
Green Chilies: 2-3, finely chopped
Ginger: 1-inch piece, finely chopped
Salt: To taste
Oil/Ghee: For cooking (about 4-5 tbsp)

Process


Prepare the Batter

1. Soak the Urad Dal:
Wash and soak the urad dal in water for about 4-5 hours, or overnight.

2. Grind the Dal:
Drain the soaked dal and grind it into a smooth, thick batter using minimal water. The consistency should be similar to that of idli batter.

3. Mix with Rice Rava:
In a large mixing bowl, combine the urad dal batter with rice rava. If using store-bought rice rava, rinse it once before mixing it into the batter. Add salt, cumin seeds, chopped green chilies, and ginger. Mix well. The batter should be thick but spreadable. Let it rest for about 30 minutes.

Cook the Dibba Rotti

4. Heat the Pan:
Use a heavy-bottomed pan or cast iron skillet (traditionally, a thick iron kadai is used). Add about 2-3 tbsp of oil or ghee to the pan and heat it over medium heat.

5. Spread the Batter:
Once the oil is hot, pour a thick layer of batter (about 1 inch thick) into the pan. Spread it gently with a ladle or spoon to even out the surface.

6. Cover and Cook:
Cover the pan with a lid and cook on low heat for 10-15 minutes, or until the bottom of the rotti turns golden brown and crispy.

7. Flip and Cook the Other Side:
Carefully flip the dibba rotti using a spatula. Add a little more oil or ghee around the edges if needed. Cover and cook the other side for another 10-15 minutes until it's crispy and golden brown as well.

Serve

8. Serve Hot:
Once both sides are crispy and cooked through, remove the dibba rotti from the pan. Cut it into wedges or pieces and serve hot with coconut chutney, tomato chutney, or any pickle of your choice.


Enjoy your Dibba Rotti! This dish is a delightful combination of a crispy outer layer and a soft, fluffy interior, making it a perfect comfort food.
